What is Attrition?

Employee attrition is the natural process by which employees leave the workforce. It can occur due to retirement, voluntary or involuntary reasons, lack of professional growth, hostile work environment, or declining confidence in the company's market value.

Types of Attrition:

  1. Attribution due to retirement
  2. Voluntary attribution
  3. Involuntary attribution
  4. Internal attribution
  5. Demographic-specific attribution
  6. Lack of professional growth
  7. Hostile work environment
  8. Declining confidence in the companyâ€™s market value
  9. Weak leadership

Attrition Measures:

Attrition measures how many people left a company/office/department compared to the average number of people employed in that year.

Example:

Employee started new year - 1000
People leave - 200
Hired new people - 400
Final head count in a year - 1400
Average number of employees - (1000 + 1400) / 2 = 1200
Attrition rate = (200 / 1200) * 100 = 16.66%

Problem Statement

Predicting employee attrition using supervised learning.

Scope of Project

The project focuses on leveraging machine learning, particularly supervised learning techniques, to analyze and predict employee attrition within organizations. Recognizing the significance of human resources in driving competitive advantages, the aim is to move beyond subjective decision-making in HR management and utilize objective data analysis.

By examining various factors that influence employee attrition, the goal is to identify the primary causes behind employees leaving a company and create a predictive model to forecast whether a particular employee might depart. Using a real dataset provided by IBM analytics, comprising approximately 1470 samples and 35 features, the project employs machine learning algorithms for training and testing.

Through this analysis, the project demonstrates the utility of ML-driven predictive models in HR decision-making, allowing organizations to proactively address employee retention based on data-driven insights.

About Dataset

Dataset: Employee Data Attrition

The dataset comprises 1470 records with 35 features, among which 26 are numerical attributes and 9 are categorical attributes. Within the numerical attributes, some fields store categorical information represented by numerical labels. To facilitate comprehensive analysis, these numerical labels will be substituted with corresponding categorical values. This data structure presents an opportunity to enhance the interpretability of the dataset by transforming certain numeric representations into more meaningful categorical labels.

Summary

The organization experiences an overall employee attrition rate of 16.12%. Notably, male employees constitute a larger segment than females by over 20%, despite which the attrition rate among males surpasses that of females.

Most employees fall within the age range of 30 to 40, showing a decreasing attrition trend with increasing age. Employees who travel frequently exhibit higher attrition rates, while non-travelers experience the lowest. The Research & Development department has the highest employee count but the lowest attrition rate among all departments.

Machine Learning Models Performance:

  • Logistic Regression: Accuracy - 86.73%, Precision - 43.37%, Recall - 50%
  • Random Forest: Accuracy - 87.75%, Precision - 83.94%, Recall - 54.93%
  • Support Vector Machines (SVM): Accuracy - 86.73%, Precision - 43.37%, Recall - 50%

All models displayed strengths and weaknesses in identifying positive and negative instances. Further analysis and potential model optimization could enhance their capabilities, especially in correctly identifying positive instances.
